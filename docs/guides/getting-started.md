# Getting Started with POSnet

POSnet (Phobos Operational Signals Network) is a radio-powered intelligence
terminal for Project Zomboid. It turns any radio and computer into a CRT-style
command terminal that lets you monitor commodity markets, intercept signals,
run operations, and trade with contacts across the map.

---

## Requirements

You need two things to open a POSnet terminal:

1. **A radio** (any HAM radio, walkie-talkie, or military radio).
2. **A computer** -- either:
   - A desktop computer within **3 tiles** of the radio, or
   - A portable computer (laptop) in your inventory.

Both the radio and computer must be powered. Desktop computers need wall power
or a generator; portable computers run on batteries.

You also need the **AZAS Frequency Index** mod installed -- POSnet uses its
dual-band frequency system (amateur + tactical) to determine which signals
you can receive.

---

## Connecting to the Terminal

1. Stand near your radio and computer setup.
2. **Right-click the radio** to open the context menu.
3. Select **"Connect to POSnet Terminal"**.
4. The CRT terminal window opens.

If the option does not appear, check that a powered computer is within range
(see Troubleshooting).

---

## First Steps

When you first open the terminal, you will see the **Main Menu**. Here is
what to do first:

### 1. Check the Market Overview

Navigate to **Markets > Market Overview**. This screen shows a summary of
commodity categories, average prices, and zone pressure data. Even before
you actively gather intel, the terminal receives **ambient intelligence** --
passive data that trickles in over time from radio chatter.

### 2. Set Up Your Watchlist

From the Markets hub, open **Watchlist**. Add commodity categories you care
about (weapons, medical supplies, fuel, etc.). The watchlist tracks price
changes and alerts you when significant shifts occur.

### 3. Browse Zone Data

Each geographic zone in the world has different supply levels and price
modifiers. The Market Overview shows zone pressure -- how much supply or
demand exists in each area. Use this to plan where to scavenge or trade.

## The Discovery System

POSnet uses a progressive discovery model. Not everything is visible from
the start:

- **Ambient intel** arrives passively over approximately 30 game-minutes of
  having the terminal open or a radio on. This populates basic market data.
- **Active scanning** with a Data Recorder device reveals more detailed
  intelligence about specific categories and locations.
- **Camera workstations** and **satellite uplinks** unlock the highest tiers
  of compiled intelligence.
- **Terminal screens** appear or unlock as you gain SIGINT skill levels and
  acquire the right equipment.

Screens you cannot access yet appear greyed out with a reason (e.g.,
"Requires Data Recorder" or "SIGINT Level 3 required").

---

## Tips for Beginners

- **Leave a radio on** near your base computer. Ambient intel accumulates
  even when the terminal is closed, as long as the radio is powered and
  tuned to an AZAS frequency.
- **Check the BBS** (Bulletin Board System) for available operations and
  delivery missions. These are your primary source of income and reputation.
- **Invest in SIGINT early.** The Signal Intelligence skill gates access to
  advanced screens and boosts rewards from operations. You gain SIGINT XP
  from all intelligence-gathering activities.
- **Start with Tier I operations.** They have no cancellation penalty and
  are a safe way to learn the mission system.
- **Customise your terminal.** Open Settings to change the colour theme
  (4 options), font size (4 sizes), and window dimensions to your liking.
